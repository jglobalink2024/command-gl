# 1. Primary Customer — Boutique Consulting Firm

| **Dimension** | **Profile** |
| --- | --- |
| Firm Size | 5–50 people |
| Revenue Range | $500K – $10M ARR |
| Geography | US-primary, with LATAM and international exposure |
| AI Maturity | Early adopter — already running AI agents but without centralized management |
| Current Tools | Any combination of Claude, ChatGPT, GPT-4, Perplexity, Cursor, Make, Zapier, or multiple instances of the same tool simultaneously |
| Budget Authority | Founder, COO, or Head of Operations |

# 2. Core Pain Points

- Agents are running but nobody can see what they are doing in real time

- When an agent stalls or fails, there is no visibility into why or where

- Context does not transfer cleanly between agents — handoffs break and work gets lost

- No centralized place to assign tasks to agents, monitor progress, or intervene

- Multi-agent AI feels powerful in demos but chaotic in production

- The firm is paying for AI infrastructure but cannot prove its ROI to clients or leadership

# 3. Ideal First Customer Profile — Version A (Multi-Tool)

- Founder-led boutique consulting firm, 5–20 people

- Already paying for Claude, GPT-4, or similar — AI is not new to them

- Running at least 2 active agents or tools in production today

- Founder is technical enough to understand MCP but too busy to manage it manually

- Based in US, English-speaking, monthly SaaS buyer comfortable at $149/month

# 4. Ideal First Customer Profile — Version B (Single-Service Multi-Instance)

**This is the PRIMARY USE CASE. More common than multi-tool operators.**

- Solo founder or small firm (1–10 people) running one AI service exclusively

- Regularly operates 3–5 simultaneous tabs or sessions of the same tool (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.)

- No coordination between those instances — founder is the only bridge

- Experiencing "tab chaos" — work lives in disconnected sessions with no shared context

- Does NOT need to be running multiple tools — same-tool multi-instance is the entry pain

**Qualifying question for this profile: ****"****How many Claude (or ChatGPT) tabs do you typically have open simultaneously? How do those sessions share context with each other?****"**

# 5. Secondary Customer — AI-Enabled Service Business

Any service business that has operationalized AI agents as part of client delivery — marketing agencies, legal tech shops, financial advisory firms, research outfits — faces the same management problem at scale.

# 6. Who Is NOT The Customer

- Enterprise (100+ person firms) — they have internal DevOps and monitoring tools

- Solo operators with one agent, single session — the management problem has not appeared yet

- Technical AI builders — they build their own ops tooling

- Non-technical businesses with no existing AI agent deployment
