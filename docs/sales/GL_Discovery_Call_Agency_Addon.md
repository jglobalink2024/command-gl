# **Purpose of This Add-On**

Your COMMAND discovery calls are research-framed, zero-pitch, listen-only. That discipline does not change. This add-on gives you four passive listening triggers — signals that indicate a prospect is also a potential GL AI Agency client. You do not introduce the agency service during a COMMAND discovery call. You note the signal, and follow up separately after the call closes.

This is intelligence collection, not sales. Stay in researcher mode throughout.

# **The Core Tension to Manage**

COMMAND and the GL AI Agency serve the same buyer — but they are different offers with different entry points. During a discovery call:

- COMMAND solves: I cannot manage and see what my AI agents are doing

- GL AI Agency solves: I need research, content, and ops work produced — I cannot afford to hire

Both problems often exist in the same firm. Your job in the call is to listen for both — but only follow up on the agency signal after the COMMAND conversation is complete. Never introduce two offers in one meeting.

# **The Four AI Agency Signal Triggers**

Listen for these exact phrases or themes. When you hear one, mentally flag it. Do not pivot the conversation. Log it in your post-call synthesis.

## **Signal 1 — The Capacity Complaint**

What it sounds like:

- "We are just too small to produce everything our clients expect."

- "I spend half my week writing reports that should take an hour."

- "We had to turn down work because we did not have the bandwidth."

What it means: They need delivery capacity, not just tooling. This is an AI agency buyer.

How to log it: Note: Capacity complaint — agency signal. Follow up with GL AI Agency one-pager post-call.

## **Signal 2 — The Client Delivery Struggle**

What it sounds like:

- "Clients want more frequent updates but we cannot keep up."

- "We promised a monthly intelligence brief but we are behind every single month."

- "The work is good when we do it — we just cannot do it consistently."

What it means: Recurring deliverable production is broken. This is exactly what the GL AI Agency Starter tier solves.

How to log it: Note: Delivery consistency problem — agency Starter tier fit.

## **Signal 3 — The Revenue Ceiling Signal**

What it sounds like:

- "We cannot take on more clients without hiring, and I cannot afford to hire."

- "Our revenue is flat because we are at capacity."

- "I would grow faster but the bottleneck is production, not sales."

What it means: They understand their growth constraint. They are looking for leverage. The AI agency model is the answer — position it as adding delivery capacity without headcount.

How to log it: Note: Revenue ceiling — growth tier candidate. High-priority follow-up.

## **Signal 4 — The Founder-as-Operator Exhaustion Signal**

What it sounds like:

- "I am doing everything — the strategy and the admin and the production."

- "I did not start this firm to spend my days writing summaries."

- "I need to get out of the weeds but I cannot afford help yet."

What it means: The founder is the delivery bottleneck. This is the exact pain point the GL AI Agency was designed to remove. This is your warmest buyer.

How to log it: Note: Founder-as-operator exhaustion — highest priority agency prospect.

# **What NOT to Do During the Call**

- Do not introduce the GL AI Agency mid-call — it breaks trust and derails the COMMAND research frame

- Do not ask leading questions designed to surface agency pain — let them surface organically

- Do not combine the COMMAND pitch and the agency pitch in your follow-up email — send them separately, 2–3 days apart

- Do not offer pricing during a discovery call — for either product

# **Post-Call Action Sequence — Agency Signal Detected**

| **Timing** | **Action** | **Detail** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Day 0 | Log signal | Add to post-call synthesis: which signal triggered, exact quote or paraphrase, tier fit estimate |
| Day 1 | Send COMMAND follow-up | Standard COMMAND post-call email — no agency mention |

# **The One Sentence That Opens the Agency Conversation**

When the time is right — in the Day 3 follow-up or a subsequent call — use this framing:

*"**A lot of firms I speak with have the same ops visibility problem COMMAND solves — but they also have a delivery capacity problem on the other side. We actually run a small AI agency division at GlobaLink that handles research, reporting, and content for boutique firms. Want me to send over what that looks like?**"*

That is the full introduction. If they say yes, send the one-pager. If they say no, move on. No elaboration needed in the moment.
