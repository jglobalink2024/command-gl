Companion to the Discovery Call Playbook. This guide is for deeper interview sessions (30–45 minutes) with high-value Tier 1 prospects who have agreed to an extended research conversation. Not a first-call document.

# Purpose

The discovery call playbook covers 20-minute intro calls. This guide is for the follow-up research session — when a Tier 1 prospect invites a deeper conversation. Goal: extract enough operational detail to validate Phase 2 feature priorities and build a compelling case study template.

# Pre-Interview Brief

- Review their discovery call synthesis before the session

- Note the specific failure story they shared — return to it with more depth

- Know their firm size, agent stack, and ICP score going in

- Confirm: this session is 30–45 minutes, recorded with permission

- Deliverable: a 1-page case study draft you'll share back with them for review

# Interview Questions — Six Areas

## Area 1 — Agent Stack Depth (5 minutes)

- Walk me through every AI tool your firm has running right now — in a typical work week, what does the stack look like?

- Which of those are you most dependent on? If it disappeared tomorrow, what would break?

- Have any tools been deprecated or replaced in the last 6 months? What drove that decision?

## Area 2 — Coordination Reality (10 minutes)

- Take me through a real task that moved between two or more agents. Walk me through what that actually looked like, step by step.

- Where exactly did that handoff happen? Who or what made it happen?

- What information had to be re-entered or re-established when the task moved?

- How long did the coordination overhead take versus the actual task completion?

## Area 3 — Failure Mode Archaeology (8 minutes)

- Tell me about the worst agent failure you've had. Not the most recent — the one that cost the most.

- How long was it before you knew it had failed?

- What was the client or business impact?

- What did you change afterward — in the process, the tools, or the team?

## Area 4 — Current Workarounds (5 minutes)

- What's your best current solution for knowing what all your agents are doing at any moment?

- If you had to show a new team member 'this is how we manage our agent stack,' what would that look like?

- What does it cost you — in time, money, or reliability — to do it the current way?

## Area 5 — Ideal State (5 minutes)

- If you could design the perfect interface for managing your agent stack, what would the first screen look like?

- What would have to be true for you to trust a tool with your agent operations?

- What would make you NOT adopt a new tool, even if it looked perfect on paper?

## Area 6 — Buying Reality (5 minutes)

- When you adopt a new tool for operations, how does that decision typically happen — who's involved, what's the timeline?

- What does success look like 90 days after adopting something like COMMAND?

- Is there a budget threshold below which you don't need approval from anyone?

# Post-Interview Protocol

- Fill complete synthesis within 1 hour of session end

- Score ICP using full rubric — update Tier assignment if score changes

- Draft 1-page case study outline — share back within 72 hours for their review

- Log agency signal if detected — see GL_Discovery_Call_Agency_Addon.docx

- Identify any feature requests for Phase 2 roadmap — flag for COMMAND-0 review
