# Section 01 — Pre-Call Prep Checklist

- Review their LinkedIn profile and company website — note any AI/automation signals

- Run a quick Perplexity search on their firm — look for recent news or product launches

- Note their firm size, service type, and any AI tools mentioned publicly

- Open the post-call synthesis template (Section 07) — fill it live

- Know your goal: extract ONE usable insight about their ops, not a signed contract

- Confirm your call is NOT recorded without their permission — ask at the start

- Reminder: you are a researcher today, not a founder

**NEW v2: **If their LinkedIn or website shows only one AI tool (Claude-only, ChatGPT-only) — do NOT downgrade them. Prepare the single-service multi-instance follow-up (see Q1 below). This is the primary use case.

# Section 03 — Five Core Discovery Questions

## Q1 — Agent Inventory

**Question: ****"****Walk me through the AI tools you****'****re running right now — not what you****'****re evaluating, what****'****s actually live and being used.****"**

What to listen FOR:

- GREEN: Multiple agents named (Claude, Perplexity, Cursor, GPT-4, n8n, Zapier)

- GREEN: Operator frustration with managing multiple tabs or contexts

- GREEN: Single tool mentioned — DO NOT EXIT. Follow up immediately.

- RED: "We're mostly using ChatGPT for writing" — not the ICP

**NEW v2 — Single-Tool Follow-Up (CRITICAL): If they name only one tool, immediately ask: ****"****How many [tool name] sessions or tabs are you typically running at the same time? And how does work move between those sessions?****"**** This surfaces the single-service multi-instance pain — the primary use case.**

## Q2 — Coordination Behavior

**Question: ****"****How do you actually move work between those tools right now? Like, if you start something in one agent and need to continue it in another — what does that look like?****"**

- GREEN: "I copy and paste context manually" — this IS the pain

- GREEN: "We have someone on the team who basically manages the handoffs"

- GREEN: Any description of manual relay between tools or tabs

- GREEN (single-tool): "I just open a new tab and re-explain everything"

- RED: "They all talk to each other automatically" — indicates different stack or low maturity

## Q3 — Failure Mode

**Question: ****"****Tell me about a time something went wrong with your agent setup — when it stalled, dropped context, or just didn****'****t work the way you expected.****"**

- GREEN: Specific failure story — agent went silent, work was lost, client was affected

- GREEN: "I found out two days later" — visibility gap confirmed

- GREEN: Emotional language — frustration, embarrassment, time lost

- RED: "Nothing's really gone wrong" — either low usage or defensive

## Q4 — Quantify the Cost

**Question: ****"****How much time do you think you or your team spend managing the agents themselves — versus actually using their output?****"**

- GREEN: Any number above "a few minutes" — even "30 minutes a day" validates the TAM

- GREEN: "More than I'd like to admit" — self-awareness of waste

- GREEN: "We hired someone to monitor it" — highest-signal response possible

- RED: "It's pretty automated, honestly" — not the ICP

## Q5 — Vision

**Question: ****"****If you had a single place to see everything your agents were doing — status, task queue, handoff state — what would that change for you?****"**

- GREEN: "I'd know what's actually running" — visibility resonance

- GREEN: "I could actually delegate to them properly" — ROI framing

- GREEN: "I'd sleep better" — emotional validation

- RED: Blank or confused response — they may not have the problem yet

# Section 04 — Deflect-Back Protocol

If they ask "What is COMMAND?" or "What are you building?" mid-call:

*"**I**'**ll tell you exactly what I**'**m building at the end — but I want to make sure I actually understand your situation first, or else what I tell you won**'**t be relevant. Can I ask you one more thing?**"*

At 18 minutes, close the call:

*"**This has been incredibly useful — you**'**ve confirmed several things I was only guessing at. I**'**m building something that addresses exactly what you described. It works with whatever AI tools you**'**re already running — Claude, ChatGPT, or any combination. That**'**s intentional. It**'**s early stage and I**'**m selective about who I bring in. Would it be okay if I sent you a one-pager on what I**'**m building and followed up in a week or two to see if it**'**s relevant to where you are?**"*

**NEW v2: **The phrase "whatever AI tools you're already running — Claude, ChatGPT, or any combination" is the model-agnostic inoculation. It prevents the "I'll just use Claude Projects" objection before it surfaces.
