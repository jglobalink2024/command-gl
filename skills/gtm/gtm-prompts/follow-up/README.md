# Follow-Up Prompts

The fortune is in the follow-up. Bumps, sequences, and re-engagement that actually get replies.

---

## Email Sequence Builders

### Generate a 5-Touch Sequence

```
Create a 5-email outbound sequence for [PERSONA] at [COMPANY TYPE].

Email 1: Initial outreach
Email 2: Follow-up (3 days later)
Email 3: Value add (5 days later)
Email 4: Different angle (7 days later)
Email 5: Breakup (10 days later)

Context:
- Product: [WHAT YOU SELL]
- Value prop: [KEY BENEFIT]
- Tone: [SELECT]

For each email:
- Subject line
- Body (under 75 words)
- Goal of that touch
```

---

### Generate a Nurture Sequence

For leads that aren't ready now.

```
Create a 4-email nurture sequence for prospects who said "not now."

Timing: 1 email per month for 4 months

Each email should:
- Provide standalone value (not just "checking in")
- Include a resource, insight, or data point
- Soft CTA to re-engage
- Not feel like sales follow-up

My industry: [INDUSTRY]
My solution: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Tone: [SELECT]
```

## Bump Emails

### The Simple Bump

When they haven't responded.

```
Write a follow-up email to [PERSON] who didn't respond to my previous email.

Previous email: [PASTE OR SUMMARIZE]
Days since: [NUMBER]

Rules:
- Under 25 words
- Don't restate everything
- Add one small thing (insight, question, or angle)
- Make it easy to respond
```

**Example outputs:**

> [Name] — bumping this up. Worth a quick chat?

> Did this land? Happy to share how [similar company] approached this.

> [Name] — circling back. Timing not right, or just buried?

---

### The Value-Add Bump

Add something useful.

```
Write a follow-up email that adds value instead of just "checking in."

Context:
- Original email was about: [TOPIC]
- I can share: [RESOURCE, INSIGHT, OR DATA]

Rules:
- Lead with the value
- Brief reminder of why you're reaching out
- Under 50 words
```

**Example output:**

> [Name] — came across this report on [topic] and thought of our conversation.
>
> [Link or key stat]
>
> Still think there's a fit here. Open to a quick chat this week?

---

### The Breakup Email

Last touch before you stop.

```
Write a breakup email to [PERSON] who hasn't responded to [NUMBER] previous emails.

Rules:
- Acknowledge you've been persistent
- Give them an easy out
- Leave the door open
- Don't be passive-aggressive
- Under 50 words
```

**Example outputs:**

**Direct:**
> [Name] — I've reached out a few times with no luck. I'll assume timing isn't right.
>
> If that changes, I'm here. Either way, good luck with [initiative].

**Casual:**
> hey [name] — clearly my timing is off. i'll stop filling your inbox.
>
> if [problem] ever becomes a priority, my info's below. no hard feelings either way.

**With humor:**
> [Name] — going to take a hint and stop emailing.
>
> If you ever want to talk about [topic], you know where to find me. Until then, I'll be here, improving my email open rates elsewhere.

## Re-Engagement

### Closed Lost Re-Engagement

```
Write an email to re-engage [PERSON] who went with a competitor [TIMEFRAME] ago.

Context:
- They chose: [COMPETITOR]
- Common issue with that choice: [PROBLEM]
- What's changed with us: [UPDATE]

Rules:
- Don't trash the competitor
- Lead with curiosity
- Offer a no-pressure check-in
- Under 75 words
```

---

### Ghosted After Demo Re-Engagement

```
Write an email to [PERSON] who went silent after our demo [WEEKS] ago.

Demo context: [WHAT YOU SHOWED THEM]
Last communication: [WHAT THEY SAID]

Rules:
- Don't guilt them
- Reference something specific from the demo
- Offer an easy path forward
- Under 60 words
```

**Example output:**

> [Name] — we chatted about [specific topic] a few weeks back and then things went quiet.
>
> Totally understand if priorities shifted. If it's helpful, happy to do a quick recap or answer any questions that came up internally.
>
> Either way, no pressure.

---

### After Long Gap Re-Engagement

```
Write an email to [PERSON] I haven't contacted in [MONTHS].

Context:
- Last touchpoint: [WHAT HAPPENED]
- What's new: [PRODUCT UPDATE, NEWS, OR REASON TO RECONNECT]

Rules:
- Acknowledge the gap
- Give a legitimate reason for reaching out
- Not "just checking in"
- Under 75 words
```

## Post-Meeting Follow-Ups

### After Discovery Call

```
Write a follow-up email after a discovery call with [PERSON].

Call summary:
- Their challenges: [LIST]
- Their timeline: [URGENCY]
- Next steps discussed: [WHAT WAS AGREED]

Include:
- Thanks (brief)
- Recap of key points
- Confirm next steps
- Attach/link anything promised
- Under 150 words
```

---

### After Demo

```
Write a follow-up email after demoing to [PERSON] and their team.

Demo context:
- Attendees: [WHO WAS THERE]
- Features they were most interested in: [LIST]
- Concerns raised: [LIST]
- Agreed next steps: [WHAT WAS DISCUSSED]

Include:
- Personalized recap (not generic)
- Address concerns mentioned
- Clear next step with specific date/time
- Any resources that would help
```

---

### After No-Show

```
Write a follow-up email to [PERSON] who didn't show up to our scheduled call.

Tone: Understanding, not annoyed

Include:
- Acknowledge things happen
- Offer to reschedule
- Provide alternative times
- Very brief (under 50 words)
```

**Example output:**

> [Name] — missed you on our call today. No worries, these things happen.
>
> Happy to reschedule whenever works. Here's my calendar: [link]
>
> Or just reply with a time that's better.

## Follow-Up by Persona

### Following Up with Executives

```
Write a follow-up to [EXEC TITLE] who hasn't responded.

Rules specific to executives:
- Extremely brief (under 40 words)
- Lead with impact, not features
- Suggest a delegate if appropriate
- One clear ask
```

---

### Following Up with Technical Buyers

```
Write a follow-up to [TECHNICAL ROLE] who evaluated our product.

Include:
- Reference specific technical points discussed
- Offer additional technical resources
- Address any integration concerns
- Suggest a technical deep-dive
```

---

### Following Up with Champions

```
Write a follow-up to [PERSON] who likes our solution but needs to sell internally.

Include:
- Arm them with talking points
- Offer to help with internal pitch
- Provide materials they can share
- Make their job easier
```

## Follow-Up Frameworks

### The 3x3 Follow-Up Method

```
I need to follow up with [PERSON] who didn't respond.

Use the 3x3 method:
- 3 different angles (value, social proof, different benefit)
- 3 days apart
- Each under 50 words

Context:
- Original email: [SUMMARY]
- Product: [WHAT YOU SELL]
- Their likely objection: [GUESS]

Generate all 3 follow-ups.
```

Create a multi-channel follow-up sequence for [PERSON]:

Touch 3: Email bump (Day 4)
Touch 4: LinkedIn message (Day 6)
Touch 5: Phone/voicemail (Day 8)
Touch 6: Final email (Day 10)

Write copy for each touch.

Context: [YOUR SITUATION]
Tone: [SELECT]
