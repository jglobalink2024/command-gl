# Outreach Prompts

Cold email, LinkedIn, and voice note prompts in every tone. Pick what fits your brand and buyer.

---

## Cold Email — By Tone

### Direct (No Fluff)

Gets to the point immediately. Best for busy executives.

```
Write a cold email to [PERSON], [TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Tone: Direct. No fluff. Respect their time.

Context:
- They [RELEVANT SIGNAL: just raised funding / hired for X / launched Y]
- We help [TYPE OF COMPANY] with [PROBLEM]
- Desired outcome: [SPECIFIC RESULT]

Rules:
- Under 75 words
- No "hope this finds you well"
- No buzzwords
- Clear ask at the end
- Subject line under 5 words
```

**Example output:**

> Subject: Quick question
>
> [Name] —
>
> Saw you're scaling the sales team post-Series B.
>
> We help B2B SaaS companies get 50+ qualified leads/month without hiring SDRs. AI does the research, human AE reviews everything.
>
> Worth 15 minutes to see if it fits?
>
> [Your name]

---

### Consultative (Question-Led)

Opens with curiosity. Best for complex sales.

```
Write a cold email to [PERSON], [TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Tone: Consultative. Lead with a question. Show genuine curiosity about their situation.

Context:
- Company info: [WHAT YOU KNOW]
- We help with: [PROBLEM AREA]
- Common challenge for their type: [PAIN POINT]

Rules:
- Start with an observation, then a question
- No pitch in first email
- Goal is to start a conversation, not sell
- Under 100 words
```

**Example output:**

> Subject: Question about [Company]'s outbound
>
> [Name] —
>
> I noticed you're hiring 3 SDRs right now. Curious: is that to increase volume, or are you finding quality is the bigger issue?
>
> Asking because we work with a lot of Series A-B companies navigating this exact tradeoff.
>
> Either way, happy to share what's working for similar teams if useful.
>
> [Your name]

---

### Challenger (Share an Insight)

Provokes thinking. Best for differentiated products.

```
Write a cold email to [PERSON], [TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Tone: Challenger. Share a contrarian insight. Make them think differently.

Context:
- Industry assumption to challenge: [COMMON BELIEF]
- Our contrarian view: [DIFFERENT PERSPECTIVE]
- Evidence or data point: [PROOF]

Rules:
- Lead with the insight, not with you
- Be confident but not arrogant
- End with a soft question
- Under 100 words
```

**Example output:**

> Subject: SDR math is broken
>
> [Name] —
>
> Most B2B companies hire SDRs to scale outbound. But the math doesn't work for pre-Series B:
>
> $80K/year fully loaded. 6-month ramp. 40% turnover.
>
> We're seeing more companies skip SDR hiring entirely—using AI for research and fractional AEs for qualification.
>
> Same pipeline, fraction of the cost.
>
> Curious if this matches what you're seeing?
>
> [Your name]

---

### Casual (Human, Conversational)

Feels like a message from a friend. Best for SMB/startup buyers.

```
Write a cold email to [PERSON], [TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Tone: Casual. Like you're emailing a colleague, not a prospect. Conversational, no corporate speak.

Context:
- Why you're reaching out: [TRIGGER]
- What you might help with: [GENERAL AREA]
- Something specific about them: [PERSONALIZATION]

Rules:
- Write like you talk
- Lowercase subject line is fine
- Short sentences
- No jargon
- Under 60 words
```

**Example output:**

> Subject: random question
>
> hey [name] —
>
> saw your post about scaling sales without scaling headcount. resonated.
>
> we're building something in that space — ai that actually does the research, real humans who review it.
>
> not sure if you're even looking for tools right now but figured i'd reach out.
>
> open to a quick chat?

---

### Formal (Corporate-Friendly)

Professional tone for enterprise buyers. Best for regulated industries.

```
Write a cold email to [PERSON], [TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Tone: Formal and professional. Appropriate for enterprise/corporate environments.

Context:
- Company: [DETAILS]
- Your company: [WHO YOU ARE]
- Value prop: [WHAT YOU OFFER]

Rules:
- Professional greeting and sign-off
- Clear and structured
- Mention relevant credentials or clients
- Under 120 words
```

**Example output:**

> Subject: Lead Generation Partnership Inquiry — [Your Company]
>
> Dear [Name],
>
> I hope this message finds you well. I'm reaching out from [Your Company], where we work with B2B technology firms to accelerate pipeline development.
>
> Given [Company]'s recent expansion and focus on [specific area], I thought it worthwhile to introduce our AI-assisted lead qualification service, which has helped companies like [Client 1] and [Client 2] increase qualified opportunities by 40%.
>
> I would welcome the opportunity to schedule a brief call to explore potential alignment.
>
> Best regards,
> [Your name]
> [Title]

### Founder-to-Founder (Peer Level)

No sales speak. One builder to another.

```
Write a cold email from a founder to another founder.

Tone: Peer-to-peer. No sales language. One person building something talking to another.

Context:
- My company: [WHAT YOU'RE BUILDING]
- Their company: [WHAT THEY'RE BUILDING]
- Shared challenge: [COMMON PROBLEM]

Rules:
- Be real about what you're doing
- Reference shared experiences
- No pitch, just connection
- Ask for advice or input, not a meeting
- Under 80 words
```

**Example output:**

> Subject: founder question
>
> [Name] —
>
> Building something in the sales dev space. Noticed you've scaled [Company] through a similar go-to-market motion.
>
> Curious how you thought about outbound early on — did you hire SDRs or find another way?
>
> Not trying to sell you anything. Genuinely trying to learn from people further along.
>
> Either way, congrats on what you're building.
>
> [Your name]

## Cold Email — By Signal

### After Funding Announcement

```
Write a cold email to [PERSON] at [COMPANY] who just raised [AMOUNT] in [ROUND].

Angle: Funding = growth mode. They'll need pipeline fast but hiring takes time.

Include:
- Congratulate briefly (1 sentence max)
- Acknowledge their likely priority (scaling)
- Position your solution as speed to result
- Clear CTA

Tone: [DIRECT / CASUAL / etc.]
```

---

### After Executive Hire

```
Write a cold email to [NEW HIRE NAME], new [TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Angle: New executives have a ~90 day window to make an impact. They're evaluating everything.

Include:
- Acknowledge the transition
- Reference what's typically top of mind for new [TITLE]s
- Offer a quick win or insight
- Low-commitment CTA

Tone: [SELECT TONE]
```

---

### After Product Launch

```
Write a cold email to [PERSON] at [COMPANY] who just launched [PRODUCT/FEATURE].

Angle: Post-launch = need to drive adoption/sales. Marketing and sales priorities shift.

Include:
- Reference the launch specifically
- Connect to a problem the launch might create or solve
- Position how you help
- CTA

Tone: [SELECT TONE]
```

---

### After Job Posting

```
Write a cold email to [PERSON] at [COMPANY] who posted a job for [ROLE].

Angle: Hiring for [ROLE] suggests they're trying to solve [PROBLEM]. Offer an alternative or complement.

Include:
- Reference the specific job posting
- Hypothesize what they're trying to solve
- Offer a different approach or faster path
- CTA

Tone: [SELECT TONE]
```

### Based on Content They Shared

```
Write a cold email to [PERSON] who posted about [TOPIC] on LinkedIn.

Angle: Reference their thinking, add to it, then bridge to your solution.

Include:
- Specific reference to their content (not generic)
- Your perspective on the topic
- How it connects to what you do
- Conversational CTA

Tone: [SELECT TONE]

Their post: [PASTE OR SUMMARIZE]
```

## LinkedIn Messages

### Connection Request with Note

```
Write a LinkedIn connection request to [PERSON], [TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Context: [WHY YOU'RE REACHING OUT]

Rules:
- Under 300 characters (LinkedIn limit)
- No pitch
- Give a reason to connect
- Sound human
```

**Examples by tone:**

**Direct:**
> [Name] — saw you're building out sales at [Company]. Working on something similar. Would be good to connect.

**Casual:**
> hey [name] — your post on [topic] was solid. always looking to connect with people thinking about this stuff.

**Consultative:**
> [Name] — curious how you're thinking about [challenge]. Researching this space and your perspective would be valuable.

- Goal: Start a conversation

Rules:
- Under 100 words
- Don't pitch immediately

### LinkedIn DM — Follow Up

```
Write a follow-up LinkedIn DM to [PERSON] who didn't respond to my first message.

First message sent: [PASTE OR SUMMARIZE]
Days since: [NUMBER]

Rules:
- Short (under 50 words)
- Add value or new angle
- Don't guilt them
- Easy to respond to
```

## Voice Note Scripts

### Introduction Voice Note

```
Write a voice note script (60 seconds max) to introduce myself to [PERSON] at [COMPANY].

Context:
- Signal: [WHAT TRIGGERED THIS]
- My solution: [WHAT I OFFER]
- Goal: Get a response

Rules:
- Conversational, not scripted-sounding
- Include their name and company
- Reference something specific
- Clear ask at the end
- Tone: [SELECT]
```

**Example output:**

> Hey [Name], this is [Your name].
>
> Quick note — saw [Company] just closed the Series B. Congrats, that's a big milestone.
>
> I know this stage usually means scaling pipeline fast, but ramping SDRs takes forever.
>
> We've been helping B2B teams get 50+ qualified leads a month using AI research and human review — basically an SDR team without the hire.
>
> Might not be what you need right now, but if you're exploring options, I'd love to chat for 15 minutes.
>
> Anyway, congrats again. Let me know.

---

### Follow-Up Voice Note

```
Write a follow-up voice note script to [PERSON] who didn't respond to my email.

Previous outreach: [SUMMARY]
Days since: [NUMBER]

Rules:
- Under 45 seconds
- Reference previous touch lightly
- Add new value or angle
- Very low pressure
```
