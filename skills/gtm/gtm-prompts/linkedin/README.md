# LinkedIn Prompts

Posts, comments, DMs, and profile optimization. Build your personal brand while generating pipeline.

---

## LinkedIn Posts

### Thought Leadership Post

```
Write a LinkedIn post about [TOPIC] that positions me as knowledgeable without being preachy.

My perspective: [YOUR TAKE]
My audience: [WHO FOLLOWS YOU]
Tone: [CASUAL / PROFESSIONAL / PROVOCATIVE]

Rules:
- Hook in first line
- Line breaks for readability
- No more than 150 words
- End with engagement prompt (question or take)
- No hashtags unless requested
```

---

### Observation Post

Share what you're noticing without being salesy.

```
Write a LinkedIn post that shares an observation about [TOPIC/TREND].

Format:
- Open with what I'm noticing
- Give 2-3 specific examples or data points
- Share my take on why this matters
- End with a question to the audience

Rules:
- Don't mention my product
- Sound like a practitioner, not a marketer
- Under 200 words
```

---

### Contrarian Take Post

Challenge conventional wisdom.

```
Write a LinkedIn post with a contrarian take on [COMMON BELIEF].

Structure:
- Open with the conventional wisdom
- Explain why I disagree
- Give evidence or reasoning
- Acknowledge nuance
- Invite discussion

Rules:
- Be confident but not arrogant
- Don't be contrarian just to be contrarian
- Under 175 words
```

**Example topic:** "Cold email is dead"

**Example output:**

> "Cold email is dead."
>
> I hear this constantly. And yet...
>
> Last month I sent 100 emails. 22 replied. 8 booked calls.
>
> The emails that worked weren't spray and pray. They were:
> - Researched (I knew something specific about them)
> - Relevant (I had a reason to reach out)
> - Respectful (short, clear, easy to respond to)
>
> Cold email isn't dead. Lazy email is.
>
> The people saying "cold email doesn't work" usually mean "my cold emails don't work."
>
> What's your experience?

### Personal Story Post

Build connection through authenticity.

```
Write a LinkedIn post sharing a personal story about [EXPERIENCE].

Story: [WHAT HAPPENED]
Lesson: [WHAT I LEARNED]
Relevance: [WHY IT MATTERS TO MY AUDIENCE]

Rules:
- Be vulnerable but not oversharing
- Connect to professional context
- Don't make it a humble brag
- Under 200 words
```

```
Why it matters: [IMPLICATIONS]

Format:

- Give context
```

### Framework Post

Share a useful mental model.

```
Write a LinkedIn post sharing a framework I use for [PROBLEM/SITUATION].

Framework: [DESCRIBE IT]
When to use it: [CONTEXT]
Example: [HOW I APPLIED IT]

Format:
- Hook with the problem

```

### Asking for Help Post

Leverage your network authentically.

```
Write a LinkedIn post asking my network for [HELP/ADVICE/INTROS].

What I need: [SPECIFIC ASK]
Context: [WHY I'M ASKING]
What's in it for them: [RECIPROCITY]
```

## LinkedIn Comments

### Thoughtful Comment

Add value to someone else's post.

```
Write a comment on this LinkedIn post that adds value:

Post: [PASTE OR SUMMARIZE THE POST]

Rules:
- Don't just agree ("Great post!")
- Add a new perspective, example, or question
- Be genuine, not sycophantic
- Under 50 words
- No self-promotion
```

---

### Comment to Get Noticed

Strategic commenting on target accounts' posts.

```
[PERSON] at [TARGET COMPANY] posted this:

[PASTE POST]

Write a comment that:
- Adds genuine value
- Shows my expertise subtly
- Might prompt them to check my profile
- Isn't obviously trying to sell

My expertise: [RELEVANT AREA]
```

---

### Comment That Sparks Conversation

```
This post is getting engagement:

[PASTE POST]

Write a comment that:
- Offers a slightly different perspective
- Invites discussion
- Is respectful but not boring
- Might get replies

Under 75 words.
```

## LinkedIn DMs

### DM After They Engaged With Your Content

```
Write a LinkedIn DM to [PERSON] who [LIKED / COMMENTED ON] my post about [TOPIC].

Goal: Start a conversation without being salesy

Rules:
- Thank them naturally
- Reference what they said/did specifically
- Ask a genuine question
- Don't pitch
- Under 50 words
```

---

### DM After You Engaged With Their Content

```
Write a LinkedIn DM to [PERSON] after I've been commenting on their posts for [TIMEFRAME].

Goal: Move from commenter to conversation

Rules:
- Reference shared interest
- Be direct about wanting to connect
- Don't pitch
- Under 60 words
```

---

### DM to Ask for Advice

Position yourself as someone worth helping.

```
Write a LinkedIn DM to [PERSON] who is [SENIOR IN MY FIELD].

I want to ask about: [SPECIFIC TOPIC]
Why them: [WHAT THEY'VE DONE/SAID]

Rules:
- Be specific, not "pick your brain"
- Show you've done homework
- Make it easy to say yes
- Under 75 words
```

## LinkedIn Profile

### Headline Optimization

```
Write 5 LinkedIn headline options for someone who:

Role: [CURRENT TITLE]
Audience: [WHO I WANT TO ATTRACT]
Differentiator: [WHAT MAKES ME UNIQUE]

Requirements:
- Under 120 characters
- Clear value proposition
- Not just job title
- Mix of professional and human
```

**Example outputs:**

> AE @ [Company] | Helping B2B teams build pipeline without the SDR hire

> Founder @ Prospeda | AI sales development without the fluff

> Sales Leader | I write about what actually works in B2B

---

```
Write a LinkedIn About section for:

What I do: [PROFESSIONAL FOCUS]
Who I help: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
Point of view: [WHAT I BELIEVE]
Personal touch: [SOMETHING HUMAN]

Requirements:
- First person
- Hook in first line (it's the preview)
- Under 200 words
- Include CTA (link, contact, etc.)
- Tone: [SELECT]
```

## Content Planning

### Weekly LinkedIn Content Plan

```
Create a 5-day LinkedIn content plan for me:

My focus area: [TOPIC/INDUSTRY]
My audience: [WHO]
My goal: [AWARENESS / LEADS / HIRING / etc.]
Tone: [SELECT]

For each day:
- Post type (insight, story, question, etc.)
- Topic
- Hook/first line
- Brief outline

Monday through Friday.
```
