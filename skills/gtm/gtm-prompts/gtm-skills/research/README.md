# Research Prompts

Deep research on companies, people, and markets. Use before outreach, meetings, or strategy sessions.

---

## Company Research

### Quick Company Overview

Get the essentials in 30 seconds.

```
Give me a quick overview of [COMPANY]:
- What they do (one sentence)
- How they make money
- Company size (employees, funding if startup)
- Key competitors

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
```

---

### Deep Company Research

Full research brief for high-value targets.

```
Research [COMPANY] thoroughly and provide:

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW
- What they do and who they serve
- Business model and pricing (if public)
- Founding story and mission

2. FINANCIALS & GROWTH
- Funding history (rounds, investors, amounts)
- Revenue estimates if available
- Recent growth signals (hiring, expansion, product launches)

3. LEADERSHIP
- CEO and founders (background, LinkedIn)
- Key executives relevant to [YOUR PRODUCT AREA]
- Board members or notable investors

4. TECHNOLOGY
- Tech stack (check BuiltWith, Wappalyzer, job postings)
- Key integrations or platforms they use
- Technical decisions that might matter

5. RECENT NEWS
- Press releases last 6 months
- Product launches or pivots
- Executive changes

6. PAIN POINTS (HYPOTHESIZED)
- Based on their stage, industry, and recent moves
- What problems likely keep leadership up at night?

7. OUTREACH ANGLES
- 3 specific angles to open a conversation
- What would make them care about [YOUR SOLUTION]?

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
My product/service: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
```

---

### Company Research from Job Postings

Extract intel from what they're hiring for.

```
Analyze these job postings from [COMPANY] and tell me:

1. What are they building or expanding?
2. What skills are they lacking internally?
3. What tools/technologies are they using?
4. What does this suggest about their priorities?
5. How might this inform my outreach angle?

Job postings:
[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTIONS]

My product solves: [YOUR VALUE PROP]
```

---

### Funding Signal Research

When a company just raised money.

```
[COMPANY] just raised [AMOUNT] in [ROUND TYPE].

Research and tell me:
1. Who led the round? Who else participated?
2. What did they say they'll use the money for?
3. What does this funding stage typically mean for priorities?
4. What problems do companies at this stage usually face?
5. How should I position [MY SOLUTION] given this timing?

My solution: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
```

---

### Tech Stack Analysis

Understand their technical environment.

```
Based on [COMPANY]'s website, job postings, and public info, identify:

1. Frontend technologies
2. Backend/infrastructure
3. Data/analytics tools
4. Sales/marketing stack
5. Any custom or proprietary systems mentioned

Then tell me:
- What does this stack suggest about their technical maturity?
- Any integration opportunities with [MY PRODUCT]?
- Technical pain points this stack might create?

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Website: [URL]
```

## Person Research

### Quick Person Brief

Before a call or email.

```
Research [PERSON NAME] at [COMPANY]:

1. Current role and tenure
2. Previous roles (career trajectory)
3. Education
4. Content they've created (posts, podcasts, articles)
5. Topics they care about
6. Mutual connections if any

LinkedIn: [URL if available]
```

---

### Executive Profile Deep Dive

For C-level or VP targets.

```
Build a profile on [PERSON NAME], [TITLE] at [COMPANY]:

CAREER
- Current role scope and responsibilities
- Previous companies and roles
- Career pattern (operator vs. advisor, startup vs. enterprise)

PUBLIC PRESENCE
- LinkedIn activity (topics, frequency)
- Podcast appearances
- Conference talks
- Published articles or quotes

PERSONALITY INDICATORS
- Communication style based on content
- What they seem to value
- Causes or interests outside work

APPROACH RECOMMENDATIONS
- Best channel to reach them
- Topics to lead with
- What NOT to do
```

---

### Find the Right Contact

When you know the company but not the person.

```
I'm selling [PRODUCT/SERVICE] to [COMPANY].

Help me identify:
1. Who typically buys this type of solution?
2. What title should I target?
3. Who influences the decision?
4. Who might be a champion vs. economic buyer?

Based on [COMPANY]'s size ([X employees]) and industry ([Y]),
who should I reach out to first?
```

## Market Research

### Industry Pain Points

Understand common problems.

```
What are the top 5 pain points for [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY TYPE] companies?

For each pain point:
- Describe the problem specifically
- What causes it?
- How do they typically try to solve it?
- What's the cost of not solving it?

Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Company size: [SIZE RANGE]
```

---

### Competitive Landscape

Quick competitive intel.

```
Map the competitive landscape for [PRODUCT CATEGORY]:

1. List the top 10 players
2. For each: positioning, pricing tier, key differentiator
3. Who's winning and why?
4. Where are the gaps in the market?
5. How should [MY COMPANY] position against them?

My company: [NAME]
Our differentiator: [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT]
```

---

### Trend Analysis

Stay ahead of the market.

```
What are the 5 biggest trends affecting [INDUSTRY] right now?

For each trend:
- What's happening
- Why it matters
- Who's impacted most
- Implications for [MY PRODUCT CATEGORY]
- How to talk about it with prospects
```

## News & Signal Monitoring

### Trigger Event Analysis

When something happens at a target account.

```
[COMPANY] just [EVENT: hired new CRO / launched product / acquired company / etc.]

Analyze this event:
1. What does this signal about their priorities?
2. What problems might this create or solve?
3. What's the likely timeline for related decisions?
4. How should I reference this in outreach?
5. Draft a 2-sentence opener that mentions this naturally.

My solution: [WHAT YOU SELL]
```

---

### Earnings Call Insights

For public companies.

```
Analyze [COMPANY]'s recent earnings call:

1. Key metrics and how they compare to expectations
2. Strategic priorities mentioned by leadership
3. Challenges or risks they acknowledged
4. Investments they're making
5. Quotes I could reference in outreach
6. Implications for my solution: [WHAT YOU SELL]

Transcript: [PASTE or LINK]
```

---

### Press Release Analysis

Extract outreach angles from announcements.

```
Analyze this press release from [COMPANY]:

[PASTE PRESS RELEASE]

Tell me:
1. What are they announcing and why does it matter?
2. What's the subtext (what aren't they saying)?
3. What problems might this create?
4. How can I connect [MY SOLUTION] to this news?
5. Draft an outreach opener referencing this.
```

## Research Output Templates

### One-Page Account Brief

Standardized format for account research.

```
Create a one-page account brief for [COMPANY] in this format:

COMPANY SNAPSHOT
- Name, HQ, founded, employees, funding
- What they do (2 sentences max)

KEY CONTACTS
- Target contact: [Name, title, LinkedIn]
- Champion potential: [Name, title]
- Economic buyer: [Name, title]

BUSINESS CONTEXT
- Current priorities (based on news, hiring, funding)
- Likely pain points
- Competitive situation

ENGAGEMENT STRATEGY
- Best angle for first touch
- Objections to prepare for
- Proof points to reference

NEXT STEPS
- Recommended outreach sequence
- Timeline considerations
```

---

### Meeting Prep Brief

Before an important call.

```
I have a meeting with [PERSON] at [COMPANY] in [TIMEFRAME].

Create a prep brief:

1. PERSON CONTEXT
- Their role and likely priorities
- Recent LinkedIn activity or content
- Talking points they'd respond to

2. COMPANY CONTEXT
- What's happening at the company right now
- Relevant news or changes
- Competitors they're likely evaluating

3. MEETING GOALS
- What should I learn?
- What should I communicate?
- What's my ask at the end?

4. QUESTIONS TO ASK
- 5 discovery questions specific to their situation
- 2 questions about their decision process

5. OBJECTION PREP
- Likely objections and how to handle
- Questions they'll probably ask me

Meeting context: [FIRST CALL / DEMO / NEGOTIATION / etc.]
My solution: [WHAT YOU SELL]
```
